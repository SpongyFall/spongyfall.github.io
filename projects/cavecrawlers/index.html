Project Overview

What This Is

Players venture into randomly generated underground caves as dwarves, fighting mobs, discovering loot, and taking down bosses. They create builds by combining hundreds of talents and items.

My brother and I started Cave Crawlers many years ago as a passion project. I am the sole gameplay programmer with over 2500+ hours of development, while is the artist and level designer. We both do game design, evaluate player feedback, and iteratively improve the experience.

My Role

What I Worked On

Systems

As the sole programmer, I have implemented a huge range of systems over the years: the character controller, entities, enemy scaling, biome generation, talents, perks, items, XP, tasks, achievements, boss fights, and event rooms. I worked extensively with Photon multiplayer to create the server browser, lobby system, generation sync, spawning sync, player stats, physics, and more. I implemented Steamworks for lobbies and player status, as well as PlayFab for analytics and LiveOps.

Design

We use Trello to organize development, track tasks, and record new ideas. Every month we decide what needs to be finished for the next update. We follow player suggestions, bug reports, and our own ideas, steering Cave Crawlers toward the game both we and the players want to see.

Production

As a team of two, we designed the Steam page, created the trailers, and wrote the update blogs. When developing a new feature, we run through it until satisfied, then evaluate player feedback during organized Discord playtests. We keep improving systems, no matter their age, whenever we or the players find a better direction. I optimize Cave Crawlers for low-end PCs, keeping the graphics requirements and storage space minimal, so almost anyone can jump in.

Dev Notes

What I Learned

I learned how to pace myself during development, how to tell which player feedback matters, and how to add fun while balancing instead of only removing things. I learned a lot about balancing builds, boss fights, mob attacks, and progression. I also learned what players want, and I can now put myself in the player's shoes much faster. For future projects I will develop faster, focus more on polish, and release fewer bugs.
